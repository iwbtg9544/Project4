Project 4: Intrusions

Intrusions consist of a variety of attacks on systems with the main goal being to gain access to the system or directly affect the system's performance itself. These attacks often consist of RAT, Ransomware,Botnet, Zero Day Attacks. All with the intention of gaining some form of information or control over the users system for their own benefit.

A. What is an intrusion?

Intrusions are times when an unautorized person or entitiy gains access to a network or singular machine. Intrusions in the modern age usually take place from malicious malware such as worms or trojans that give access for the attacker to setup a backdoor into the system.

B. What are some recent examples of an intrusion?

Cyber attacks are constantly taking place, and while many attempts at intrusions are stopped, a potion still manage to get in. For example Internet service provider Tangerine has suffered a data breach, with the full names, dates of birth, email address and mobile phone numbers of more than 200,000 customers taken by hackers. Tangerine said in an email to customers that their personal information was disclosed in a intrusion on Feb. 18.

C. In what way can website intrusion malware impact a company's search rankings?

If your service is breached through sql injections or other means, google may determine your site is no longer safe for traffic and place warnings or automatically remove your site from listings to protect users.

D. What are the best defence mechanisms against an intrusion?

The first line of defence against intrusion is education, ensuring everyone in your company understands how to safely manage their information can be some of the best defences money can buy. However ensuring that all machines have a firewall inplace and applying an IPS machine to monitor, detect, and calssify each individual connection to prevent harmful traffic can be an extremely effective deterent aswell.

E. Online intrusions often target a country's stock exchanges - Give an example of an intrusion bringing a stock exchange down.

In 2020, the New Zeland stock exchange was unable to operate after receiving a significant DDOS attack on its servers and individual listings to the point it was unable to provide its correct listings for a period of nearly 4 days.
